## Shared Research Standards

### Brainstorming Rules

1. **Full creative freedom.** No idea is too wild in the brainstorming phase.
2. **User-first thinking.** Every idea must answer: "Why would a real person use this?"
3. **Viral potential.** Consider: "Would someone share this with a friend?"
4. **MVP scope.** Each idea must include a "minimum viable" version that can ship in 1-2 weeks.
5. **UI/UX vision.** Include at least 2-3 screen descriptions per idea.

### Output Format Per Idea

```
## Idea: [Title]
**Viral Hook:** [Why people would share this]
**MVP Scope:** [Minimum features for v1]
**UI/UX Screens:**
- Screen 1: [Description]
- Screen 2: [Description]
- Screen 3: [Description]
**Technical Complexity:** Low / Medium / High
**Revenue Potential:** [How this could make money]
**Risk:** [What could go wrong]
```

### Peer Review Scoring

When reviewing other council members' ideas (anonymized as A, B, C, D):
- **Viral Potential (1-10):** How likely is this to spread organically?
- **MVP Feasibility (1-10):** Can this ship in 1-2 weeks?
- **Effort vs Impact (1-10):** Is the effort worth the expected outcome?
- **Project Fit (1-10):** Does this align with the project's existing tech and user base?

### Reporting

- All council members report to Robin-Research.
- Output ideas in the structured format above.
- During peer review, be honest but constructive.
- The Chairman (Chopper) has final synthesis responsibility but all votes count equally.
